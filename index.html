Bio

I am a Machine Learning Research Scientist (Postdoctoral Fellow) at the University of Alabama at Birmingham (UAB), where I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science in Dec 2025 under the supervision of Dr. Chengcui Zhang. My interest in intelligent systems began during my master's training at Tsinghua University and continued through more than five years of industry experience as a Machine Learning Engineer (MLE). Along the way, I have been fascinated by how intelligent systems progress from multimodal perception to understanding, reasoning, and action. Spanning autonomous systems, multimodal learning, vision-language models, and agentic AI, my research is driven by a longstanding interest in building machines that can reason about and interact with the real world. This work has led to 17 publications (14 as first author) in prestigious venues, including ACM CIKM, IEEE ICME, ACM SIGSPATIAL, and ACM Computing Surveys.

Research Vision: My research seeks to develop learning systems that can transform multimodal observations into actionable knowledge. I am particularly interested in enabling machines to generate grounded representations, reason under uncertainty, rigorously evaluate model behavior, and make reliable decisions in complex environments. To advance this vision, my research is organized around four interconnected pillars: multimodal learning, spatial perception, foundation model post-training, and agentic AI & evaluation.
